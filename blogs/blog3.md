## Why Wrap Prices Vary So Much

### 1) Vehicle size and surface area

More panels = more vinyl + more labor. A large SUV can use nearly double the material of a compact car.

### 2) Film type and finish

Gloss and standard finishes are usually the entry point. Specialty films (satin metallic, chrome, textured, color-shift) increase material cost and install difficulty.

### 3) Coverage level

- **Full wrap**: all major exterior panels
- **Partial wrap**: selected areas only
- **Commercial graphics**: logo/message focus, lower material usage

### 4) Body complexity

Bumpers, deep curves, mirrors, and sharp contours increase install time and waste.

### 5) Prep and condition

Old paint defects, dents, trim issues, or residue can add prep hours before install.

## Use This Formula to Estimate Vinyl Size

A reliable planning method from wrap-size guides is:

**Vehicle length × 3 + extra (1.5m to 3m)**

The extra accounts for bumpers, overlap, and installation mistakes.

### Example

If your vehicle length is 4.95m:

4.95 × 3 = 14.85m

Add 3m buffer → **17.85m total**, so you plan around an **18m roll**.

---

## How to Estimate Cost Before You Request a Quote

Use this simple framework:

1. **Measure vehicle length** (meters)
2. **Estimate vinyl length needed** (length × 3 + buffer)
3. **Pick finish level** (standard vs premium/specialty)
4. **Choose coverage** (full, partial, graphics)
5. **Add prep contingency** (typically 10–20%)

This gives you a realistic budget band before final inspection.

## Full Wrap vs Partial Wrap: Which Is Better Value?

- **Full wrap** is best for total visual impact and uniform finish.
- **Partial wrap** is often best for budget-sensitive campaigns.
- **Commercial graphics** can deliver high branding ROI with lower upfront spend.

If your goal is lead generation, design clarity and route visibility often matter more than maximum coverage.

## Hidden Costs to Avoid

- Cheap film with early fade/shrink
- No warranty or weak aftercare support
- Installer not accounting for Durban heat/humidity exposure
- Underestimating downtime for fleet vehicles

Low initial pricing can become expensive if rework is needed.

## FAQ

**How long does a wrap last?**  
Typically 3–7 years depending on film quality, exposure, and maintenance.

**Is wrapping cheaper than repainting?**  
In many cases, yes for custom branding outcomes. It also allows reversibility.

**Can I wash a wrapped vehicle normally?**  
Yes, with wrap-safe cleaning methods and non-abrasive products.

**Do wraps damage paint?**  
On properly cured OEM paint, professional wrap removal should not damage paint.
